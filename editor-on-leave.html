A NOTE FROM THE DESK · LISBON

The desk is dark.

Saudade does not file a dispatch every day. The pipeline gathers, sorts, scores, writes, translates, stages and files at six hours past midnight Korean time, six days a week. On the seventh day it is silent — by design.

And sometimes the desk is dark for longer. The editor is on a train, in a country whose Wi-Fi will not behave, or on the kind of weekend that does not deserve a feed of policy news. The Atlas does not stop testing cafés because the desk is shut. The Ledger keeps counting.

This is the page you land on when the desk is dark. It is not a 404. It is not a server error. It is an editorial decision. The next dispatch is being thought about, not avoided.

If something is broken — really broken, not merely quiet — write to luiseluise0619@gmail.com. I read every message before the next filing.
