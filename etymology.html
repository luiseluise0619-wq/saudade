saudade.

/sɐwˈðaðɨ/ · 사우다지 · サウダージ

noun · feminine · Portuguese

The first definition

A nostalgic, melancholic longing for something or someone that is absent — and may never return. Often felt about places one once lived in, people one once loved, or moments one cannot recover. Untranslatable into English in a single word; often glossed as "the love that remains."

The second definition (this newspaper)

A slow newspaper for people who keep moving. Filed from Seoul, written about three cities at a time, once a day at six in the morning. The visa days you have left, the cafés the editor has actually sat in, the policy that changed while you slept.

A small timeline

  • 13c.First written attestations in Galician-Portuguese cancioneiros (songbooks).
  • 1500sWord travels with Portuguese sailors; appears in saudosismo poetry of exile.
  • 1912Aubrey Bell publishes In Portugal, popularising the word in English literary circles.
  • 1960sBossa nova ("Chega de Saudade", Vinicius / Jobim) carries the word worldwide.
  • 2026This newspaper, edited from Seoul, takes the word for its name.

Why we chose it

A digital nomad collects places like a beachcomber collects shells: useful for a month, then heavier than the bag. saudade is the word for the weight that remains. The newspaper is for the weight, not against it.
